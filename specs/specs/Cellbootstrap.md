# MP-CellBootstrapSpec — Intentional Community Formation

## Trust-Bonded Cells as the Foundation of Coherent Civilization

---

## Abstract

This specification defines how Dunbar-scale cells originate,
stabilize, and replicate through intentional cooperation.

No large-scale coherence system can emerge
without trust-bonded micro-communities.

All higher layers depend on this substrate.

---

## 1. Foundational Principle

> Coherence must be lived before it is instrumented.

Cells are formed by people who voluntarily commit
to mutual safety, transparency, and shared responsibility.

No external authority can impose this.

---

## 2. Preconditions for Cell Formation

A viable founding group must demonstrate:

- mutual material support
- conflict resolution capacity
- transparency in resource use
- willingness to be seen
- refusal of exploitative behavior

Ideology, belief, or identity alignment is insufficient.

Only behavioral trust qualifies.

---

## 3. The Mutual Responsibility Compact

Founding members explicitly agree:

- We ensure each other’s basic safety
- We surface conflicts early
- We do not conceal harm
- We share burden during overload
- We dissolve roles when necessary

This is not a legal contract.
It is a lived practice.

---

## 4. Visibility and Anti-Deception Environment

Cells operate in high-visibility modes:

- shared spaces
- open workflows
- accessible records
- relational accountability

This is not surveillance.

It is collective situational awareness.

Persistent deception is structurally difficult.

---

## 5. Self-Selection Filter

Because exploitation is low-yield,
agents seeking domination, secrecy, or manipulation
tend not to remain in cells.

Cells naturally repel bad actors.

No purging mechanism is required.

---

## 6. Growth and Fission

When relational bandwidth saturates (~100–150):

- subgroups form organically
- resources and practices are shared
- new cells are seeded
- instrumentation federates

Forced scaling is prohibited.

---

## 7. Charisma Resistance

Cells do not reward:

- performative leadership
- symbolic authority
- identity elevation
- dependency creation

Influence emerges only from reliability.

Charisma without service decays.

---

## 8. Failure Conditions

Cells fail when:

- anonymity dominates
- conflict is suppressed
- labor is coerced
- information is hoarded
- care becomes conditional

Such cells must dissolve and re-form.

---

## 9. Relationship to Higher Layers

Only stable cells may:

- adopt instrumentation
- federate intelligence
- participate in governance

Scale without cells produces domination.

---

## Summary

Civilization must grow the way forests grow:
root networks first, canopy later.

Cells are the roots.

No root system, no civilization.
